INFLATION REPORT ON APRIL, EFY 2017

Issue No. 22/2017 April EFY2017

The Consumer Price Index (CPI), a key economic statistics, is surveyed, analyzed, and
made available to data users on a monthly basis.

The year-on-year, general inflation rate (annual percentage change) in the country stood at
14.4 percent for the month of April EFY2017. A year earlier (in April EFY2016), the rate was
23.3 percent. The month-on-month general inflation rate increased by 0.1 percent for the
month of April-EFY2017. It was —0.7 percent in April EFY2016.

General inflation encompasses of two main divisions and twenty-one groups of expendi-
tures. These divisions registered the following price changes during the month of April

EFY2017 compared with April EFY2016.

2. Food & Non-alcoholic Beverages and Non-Food Inflation (Year-on-Year)

The year-on-year inflation and the month-to-month inflation rates helps to gauge the cur-
rent inflation trajectory, as they capture short-term fluctuations in prices and provides an
immediate indicator of inflationary pressures. Particularly, the month-to-month is also cru-
cial for monitoring changes in consumer prices and identifying any sudden spikes or de-
clines in inflation.

Food & Non-alcoholic Beverages index

The year-on-year inflation rate, also known as the annual inflation rate, measures the per-
centage change in overall price levels from one year to the same month in the previous year.
Accordingly, the year-on-year inflation rate of the Food and Non-alcoholic component of the
CPI, for the month of April 2017 EFY stood at 12.2%. This implies that the CPI of Food and
Non-Alcoholic Beverages has, on average, increased by 12.2% compared to April EFY2016.
The major contributing factors for the rise of food inflation for April EFY2017 was the ob-
served increase in the average prices of major food commodities such as bread and cereals
(1.4%), vegetables (11.5%), meat (14.4%), Sugar, jam, honey & chocolate (19.0%), Milk,
Cheese and Eggs (14.5%), Oils and Fats (25.6%), Fruit (14.9%), and Food products n.e.c
(20.1%).
